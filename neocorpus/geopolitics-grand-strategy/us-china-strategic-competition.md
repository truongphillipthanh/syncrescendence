# US-China Strategic Competition

**Sources**: 00114, 01689, 01921, 02064, 02182, 02184, 02200, 02332, 02334, 03940, 03942, 09616, 10010

## The Engineering State vs. the Lawyerly State

Dan Wang's framework: China is an **engineering state** — its national model produces tangible goods ("widgets"). America is a **lawyerly state** — its national model produces financial instruments and services ("dollars"). Both are engaged in "humiliating self-sabotage." The only force capable of defeating the USA is the USA itself. China possesses "amazing capability to produce widgets" while the US manufacturing base is susceptible to de-industrialization.

David Friedberg's framing: "The Internet is rising over the state in the West" while "China is increasingly dominating the physical world."

## The Insularity Thesis

Self-driving cars, humanoid robots, drones, and AI are massive national security threats. The US is unlikely to accept AI-powered drones and autonomous vehicles manufactured in China operating domestically. Each superpower will become more insular, focused on domestic manufacturing and supply chains.

Tesla-SpaceX merger speculation illustrates the pattern: CFIUS reviews foreign investments in US businesses to assess potential national security risks. Tesla's Giga Shanghai creates a national security risk if merged with SpaceX (which holds government contracts). Solution: separate Tesla International from Tesla US, then merge the US entity with SpaceX. The 50-100 year vision: Tesla provides infrastructure for the next century of AI, energy, robotics, and transportation.

## Deglobalization and the Energy Axis

Peter Zeihan's predictions: the next decade will be shaped significantly by energy — affecting globalization, warfare, and AI. China is expected to decline within the next 10 years. The world is entering a labor shortage for which no one is prepared. Future wars will primarily be over energy resources. Shrinking workforces will alter geopolitics. America's readiness for a deglobalized world is the key question.


## China's AI Strategy

China pursues the opposite strategy to Western AI labs on model weights. OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google develop closed-weight proprietary models. Chinese labs pursue open-source, open-weight models — creating more distributed advantage globally. DeepSeek's reasoning model surge and rapid releases from Chinese labs (Moonshot's Kimi K2.5, ByteDance's SeedDance 2.0) are closing the performance gap with Western labs. China is also considering massive subsidies and pursuing semiconductor independence in response to US export controls.

## American Stagnation and Renewal

Marc Andreessen frames the American Dream's stagnation through a cost crisis in housing, education, and healthcare — solvable through technology and deregulation. Chris Sununu's Balanced Budget Now movement has secured 27 of 34 states required for a constitutional convention. His prediction: plumbers and welders will be more valuable than "elite" white-collar workers in the AI era; fewer lawyers would be beneficial.

Startup societies are being built. Reindustrialization requires deregulation. There is a drive for new American Freedom Cities.
