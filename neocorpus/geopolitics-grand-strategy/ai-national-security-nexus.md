## AI as Civilizational Infrastructure

Three competing perspectives on AI's future: (1) extinction/superintelligence threat — AGI poses extinction-level risk; (2) total abundance — an era solving humanity's problems; (3) marketing hype — current enthusiasm is overblown. These frames coexist and compete for policy influence.

## The Experience Curve Replaces Moore's Law

Alex Wissner-Gross's claim: Moore's Law is no longer applicable to AI's progression. AI now follows an experience curve — solving problems in bulk across diverse fields (mathematics, science, engineering, medicine).

## US AI Leadership: GENESIS and Beyond

Trump's "One Rule Book" for AI regulation seeks federal preemption of state AI laws, DOJ empowerment to litigate AI matters, and Commerce Department funding linked to AI regulation compliance. David Sacks debates federal preemption versus state-level control, with implications for open-source innovation.

NVIDIA H200 exports approved — reshaping global chip strategy. China is considering massive subsidies and pursuing semiconductor independence in response to US export controls.

## India's Emergence

India is experiencing its "decade" of significant rise and influence. Manoj Ladwa and the India Global Forum are instrumental in showcasing India's rise.

## Surveillance, Deepfakes, and Democratic Risk

Palantir's ELITE tool for ICE identifies neighborhoods for enforcement raids — AI integrated into law enforcement at the community scale. AI influencers are creating deepfake content of celebrities. Comic-Con banned AI art following artist pushback — cultural backlash is emerging alongside adoption.

Over 50 NeurIPS 2025 papers were found to contain AI hallucinations.

## The Intelligence Tools Revolution

OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) has democratized intelligence gathering. Citizen-accessible tools: ADS-B Exchange (flight tracking), MarineTraffic (maritime), Sentinel satellite imagery, Google Earth Pro, LiveUAMap (conflict mapping), Broadcastify (radio scanning), Maltego (network analysis).
