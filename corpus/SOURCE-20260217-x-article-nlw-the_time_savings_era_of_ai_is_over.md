## TL;DR
People including non-engineers are vibe coding more; time savings isn't the most common value category for AI impact anymore; and the value people are getting from AI is increasing even more than their usage.
## Main Body
When we did our [AI ROI Study](https://aidbintel.com/research) at the end of last year, saving time was definitively the most common value proposition of AI use cases. In our January AIDB Intel - AI Usage Pulse Survey, however, it dropped to 3rd place -- suggesting people are moving up the value chain for AI.
We also saw a massive uptick in coding as the primary use case, including for non-coders. **~50% of non engineer/non-IT AI users** who said they vibe code **identified coding as their most important use case.**

## Other Interesting Stats
**+12 point value premium.** Between Dec and Jan, 71% reported increased AI usage, and 83% reported increased AI value. Even people who weren't using it more were getting more out of it.
**~38% of people are deploying agentic use cases now** (another massive jump up from what we saw in the ROI Survey)
**Execs were the highest title category when it came to agent usage**, with **57% reporting agentic use cases.** This means growth across the org is likely as they push their teams to evolve.
**ChatGPT was the most broadly used model but Claude was the #1 primary choice for 46% of users.**
## Methodology Note
This is a voluntary survey of my [@AIDailyBrief](https://x.com/@AIDailyBrief) audience -- meaning this is VERY forward looking and probably representing a pretty advanced band of AI users. It basically models where AI will be for other users in 3-6 months IMO.
