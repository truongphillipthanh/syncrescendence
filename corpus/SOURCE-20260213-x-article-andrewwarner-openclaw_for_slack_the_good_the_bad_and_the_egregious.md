# "OpenClaw for Slack" - The good, the bad, and the egregious
I had to try this "OpenClaw for Slack" app after it somehow hit $1 million within 3 hours. It's amazing, but now I have to remove it ASAP!
## Context
This post was trending when I logged into X this morning. It's what led me (and many others) to try new software named Viktor.

When I called the founder, he told me it's just like an employee of my company. Add it to my Slack. Give it tasks. Let it work.
I told him my first task for Viktor was to look up my notes on Wade Foster in Notion, and the results were helpful. He said that such a menial task was an insult. (I liked him instantly.) He said I should look for higher-level projects.
Viktor's website says it's my "most capable colleague." So I looked for projects for it.

## My Analysis
Viktor is the best work agent I ever used.
OpenClaw is still sooooooooo clunky and unreliable that I can't possibly expect our whole team to use it. Too many crashes, random results, access issues, etc.
Viktor is something non-AI enthusiasts can use and get work done.
But to live in a company Slack, the tool needs more refinement.
I would love to test it again.
Hell, I might even go back and add it now because I miss it.
But I really need my "most capable colleague" to treat my other colleagues well.
