## Proposed Additions by IIC Chain

### For Acumen (Sensing/Astute)

**19. Falsifiability**
*Question*: Can we know if this worked or failed? What would disprove our hypothesis?

Without clear success/failure criteria, sensing degrades into opinion. This lens forces articulation of what evidence would change our assessment—essential for the “civilizational sensing infrastructure” mission. Prevents confirmation bias from contaminating the intelligence feed.

**20. Information Asymmetry**
*Question*: What don’t we know? What’s hidden from view?

Every decision operates on incomplete information. This lens makes ignorance visible rather than assuming adequate knowledge. Particularly critical when processing paradigm-level sources where creators have unstated assumptions or agendas.

### For Coherence (Synthesis)

**21. Composability**
*Question*: Does this integrate cleanly with existing components? Can it be recombined?

The Flat Principle and metabolism semantics address file structure, but not semantic interoperability. A document might pass all current lenses yet remain an isolated artifact that resists integration. This lens asks whether the output can be meaningfully combined with other canonical elements.

**22. Legibility**
*Question*: Can the reasoning be traced? Is the decision process transparent to a future reader?

Coherence requires that synthesis be auditable—not just the conclusion but the path to it. Given your multi-account constellation and Oracle handoff pattern, a fresh agent needs to understand not just *what* was decided but *why*, with sufficient clarity to extend or challenge the reasoning.

### For Efficacy (Execution)

**23. Reversibility** (Bezos’s Type 1/Type 2)
*Question*: Can this be undone? What’s the cost of reversal?

Type 1 decisions (irreversible) demand more deliberation; Type 2 (reversible) should be made quickly. Your current framework evaluates decision quality but not decision stakes. This matters especially for infrastructure vs. content decisions—deleting originals after distillation is Type 1; trying a new processing pattern is Type 2.

**24. Energy Economics**
*Question*: What’s the cognitive load? Does this fit within finite attention budget?

Your AuDHD processing architecture means coherence-destruction from interruptions is expensive. Yet no lens currently evaluates whether a decision creates or consumes Principal attention. A perfectly elegant, first-principles-correct decision that requires constant monitoring fails if it depletes the bottleneck resource.

**25. Opportunity Cost**
*Question*: What are we NOT doing by doing this?

Lean addresses waste within a process; this addresses waste across processes. Every directive that issues is a directive that doesn’t. Given chronic constraint on Principal bandwidth, making opportunity cost explicit prevents over-investment in satisficing solutions when better alternatives exist.

### For Mastery (Teaching)

**26. Graceful Degradation**
*Question*: How does this behave under partial failure? Does it fail safely?

Teaching effectiveness depends on robustness to learner variation and context drift. A curriculum that works only under ideal conditions fails the teaching mission. This lens asks whether outputs remain valuable when conditions are suboptimal—a fresh Claude with partial context, a distracted reader, a degraded tool environment.

**27. Transmissibility**
*Question*: Can this be communicated to someone outside the system? Does jargon serve precision or obscure meaning?

Mastery requires externalization. The Syncrescendence vocabulary (metabolism, ledger ground truth, modal sequence) serves internal coherence but may impede transmission to those not yet inducted. This lens evaluates whether outputs are teaching-ready or require extensive context scaffolding.

### For Transcendence (Wisdom)

**28. Time Horizon Alignment**
*Question*: Does the evaluation timeframe match the decision’s impact timeframe?

Some decisions matter for decades; others for hours. Current lenses ask if something “meets the moment” but don’t evaluate whether we’re optimizing for the appropriate temporal scale. Wisdom requires matching deliberation intensity to impact duration.

**29. Optionality/Convexity**
*Question*: Does this preserve or expand future options? Is the upside unbounded while downside is capped?

Beyond antifragility (gains from disorder) lies optionality (the right but not obligation to act). This lens asks whether decisions keep doors open. Particularly relevant for Modal 1 → Modal 2 → Modal 3 transitions: infrastructure that preserves optionality enables adaptation; infrastructure that forecloses options creates lock-in.

**30. Compounding**
*Question*: Does value accumulate over time? Are there exponential returns?

The Bitter Lesson addresses scaling with compute; this addresses scaling with time. Some decisions yield one-time value; others compound. Given the civilizational sensing infrastructure framing, decisions that compound are categorically more valuable than those that don’t—yet no current lens distinguishes them.
