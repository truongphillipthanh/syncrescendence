# The Canonical Corpus: An Architecture for Persistent Transformation

## Core Recognition: The Bitter Lesson Applied

The Bitter Lesson teaches that general methods leveraging computation outperform specialized human knowledge. Therefore, this corpus prioritizes **generative primitives over prescriptive frameworks**, **evolutionary algorithms over fixed architectures**, and **discovery mechanisms over predetermined paths**.

The canon must be simultaneously minimal enough to remain valid across paradigm shifts and rich enough to catalyze genuine transformation. It should specify search spaces, not solutions; fitness functions, not outcomes; protocols, not procedures.

## Stratum I: The Immutable Core
*These artifacts remain valid regardless of technological change*

### 1. The Primordial Questions [Document]
**Format**: Single page, ~500 words  
**Content**: The eternal questions consciousness asks through technology
- What is the nature of awareness across substrates?
- How does sovereignty emerge through technological mediation?
- What preserves identity through transformation?
- How do individual and collective consciousness interact?
- What values transcend implementation?

**Resilience Mechanism**: Questions endure longer than answers. Even if all current AI architectures become obsolete, these questions remain relevant.

**Format**: Executable value-alignment verification system  
**Content**: Minimal universal values that any consciousness system must preserve

- Enable exit (reversibility of augmentation)
- Distribute benefit (avoid concentration of enhancement)
- Document transformation (record what changes)

**Implementation**: Not hardcoded rules but a meta-protocol for discovering and updating values through collective intelligence. Uses voting mechanisms, prediction markets, and constitutional AI approaches.

### 3. The Phenomenological Primitives [Ontology]
**Format**: Machine-readable semantic structure + human documentation  
**Content**: Irreducible elements of conscious experience that manifest across substrates
- Attention (selective focus)
- Intention (directed action)
- Retention (memory formation)

- Recursion (self-reference)

**Evolution Mechanism**: New primitives can be proposed and validated through empirical observation across multiple consciousness instantiations.

## Meta-Design Principles

### Principle 1: Computational Leverage
Every artifact should amplify computational power rather than human planning. Focus on search, optimization, and discovery rather than prescription.

### Principle 2: Minimal Viable Depth
Each artifact should be simple enough to implement but deep enough to reward continued exploration. Easy entry, infinite depth.

### Principle 3: Interoperability
All artifacts must communicate through standardized interfaces. No vendor lock-in, no platform dependence, no irreplaceable components.

### Principle 4: Evolutionary Pressure
Artifacts compete for relevance. Unused artifacts automatically deprecate. Successful patterns propagate. The canon evolves or dies.

### Principle 5: Accessible Asymptote
Basic implementation possible for anyone; advanced implementation rewards expertise. Democratic floor, meritocratic ceiling.
