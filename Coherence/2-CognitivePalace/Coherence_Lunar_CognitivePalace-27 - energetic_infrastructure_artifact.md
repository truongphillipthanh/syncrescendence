## **MANAGEMENT DOMAINS**

### **1. Attention Resource Development**
**Assessment Criteria**: Focus duration, concentration depth, distraction resistance, attention flexibility
**Optimization Protocol**:
- Develop sustained attention capacity through progressive focus training
- Create environmental conditions supporting rather than fragmenting attention
- Practice attention flexibility allowing appropriate shifting between focused and diffuse modes
- Build meta-attention awareness enabling conscious direction of attentional resources

### **2. Motivation System Optimization**
**Assessment Criteria**: Drive sustainability, intrinsic engagement, goal alignment, energy generation
**Optimization Protocol**:
- Identify and cultivate intrinsic motivation sources providing sustainable energy
- Align activities with personal values and interests for natural engagement
- Balance achievement motivation with process enjoyment and intrinsic satisfaction
- Develop motivation recovery systems restoring drive after periods of depletion

### **3. Creative Resource Access**
**Assessment Criteria**: Innovation frequency, inspiration accessibility, creative flow, generative capacity
**Optimization Protocol**:
- Create conditions supporting creative emergence including time, space, and mental state
- Practice divergent thinking and creative problem-solving to expand innovative capacity
- Develop systems for capturing and developing creative insights and inspiration
- Balance creative expression with practical implementation and completion

### **4. Energy Allocation Strategy**
**Assessment Criteria**: Resource distribution effectiveness, priority alignment, sustainability, recovery adequacy
**Optimization Protocol**:
- Allocate peak energy periods to highest-priority and most demanding activities
- Balance energy expenditure with recovery needs for sustainable long-term performance
- Develop energy efficiency strategies maximizing output while minimizing unnecessary depletion
- Create energy restoration practices enabling rapid recovery and renewal
