## **FRAMEWORK ELEMENTS**

### **1. Network Effect Causation**
**Application**: When analyzing complex situations with multiple influencing factors
**Decision Criteria**: Map interconnected influences rather than seeking single causes
**Usage Protocol**:
- Identify all relevant influence nodes in the situation
- Map interaction patterns and mutual influences
- Assess network effects and emergent system behaviors

### **2. Distributed Agency Recognition**
**Application**: When determining who/what has influence and responsibility
**Decision Criteria**: Look for influence emergence through relationships rather than isolated control
**Usage Protocol**:
- Map influence distribution across individuals, systems, and environmental factors
- Identify collaboration opportunities and shared responsibility patterns
- Assess leverage points where small actions create large effects

### **3. Simultaneous Influence Patterns**
**Application**: When planning interventions or analyzing change dynamics
**Decision Criteria**: Account for mutual causation and feedback loops rather than linear sequences
**Usage Protocol**:
- Identify reciprocal influence relationships and feedback loops
- Plan interventions accounting for simultaneous rather than sequential effects
- Monitor for unexpected interaction effects and emergent outcomes

### **4. Meaning-Moves-Matter Integration**
**Application**: When considering how consciousness and intention affect physical reality
**Decision Criteria**: Recognize downward causation from higher-order patterns to lower-level components
**Usage Protocol**:
- Assess how mental models and intentions shape action and perception
- Identify meaning-making patterns affecting behavior and outcomes
- Plan interventions leveraging consciousness change for system transformation
