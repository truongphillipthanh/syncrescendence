Alexei "Lex" Fridman (born in 1983) is an American computer scientist, podcaster, and artificial intelligence (AI) researcher. While he conducts research at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), he is best known for his popular YouTube series and podcast, The Lex Fridman Podcast. 
Background and education
- Early life: Born in the Soviet Union (modern-day Tajikistan), Fridman and his family later moved to the United States. His father, Alexander Fridman, is a plasma physicist and professor at Drexel University.
- Education: Fridman earned his Bachelor's, Master's, and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science from Drexel University, completing his doctorate in 2014.
- Research: He is a research scientist at MIT, where his work focuses on "human-centered artificial intelligence" in the context of autonomous vehicles. His research explores human-robot collaboration, machine learning, and computer vision. 
The Lex Fridman Podcast
Fridman's podcast began in 2018, originally titled The Artificial Intelligence Podcast, before changing to its current name in 2020. 
- Content: The podcast features long-form, in-depth conversations with a wide range of guests, including prominent figures from academia, business, sports, and entertainment. Topics often blend scientific and philosophical themes, such as technology, consciousness, love, and the future of humanity.
- Interview style: Fridman is known for his calm, empathetic, and sometimes earnest interview style. He approaches sensitive topics and high-profile guests with a polite and questioning demeanor, which fans credit for creating a productive conversational space.
- Notable guests: He has interviewed a broad spectrum of influential individuals, including Elon Musk, Mark Zuckerberg, Vladimir Putin, and Kanye West.
