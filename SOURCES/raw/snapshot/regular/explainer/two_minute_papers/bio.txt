Dr. Károly Zsolnai-Fehér is the founder and enthusiastic host of the popular YouTube channel, Two Minute Papers, which distills complex scientific research into easily understandable videos. The channel's well-known catchphrase, "What a time to be alive!," captures Zsolnai-Fehér's excitement for cutting-edge technological advancements in the fields of AI, machine learning, and computer graphics. 

Who is Károly Zsolnai-Fehér?
- Background: Zsolnai-Fehér is a research scientist whose work is primarily focused on computer graphics and machine learning. He earned his Ph.D. from TU Wien (Vienna University of Technology), where he also continues to be affiliated.
- Role on Two Minute Papers: As the channel's creator, he is responsible for every step of the video production process, from researching the latest scientific papers to recording and editing the videos. His energetic presentation style and ability to simplify dense technical concepts are hallmarks of the channel's success. 

What is Two Minute Papers?
- Purpose: Founded in 2006, Two Minute Papers aims to make complex and exciting research accessible to a general audience, not just experts. By showcasing the latest breakthroughs, the channel seeks to show the public how cool science and research truly are.
- Content: The videos cover a wide range of topics, including AI and deep learning, advanced simulations for things like fluids and smoke, and motion tracking for digital characters. While the original videos were very short, the length has evolved to provide more comprehensive overviews.
- Reach: The channel has attracted a large following of "Fellow Scholars" (as Zsolnai-Fehér refers to his audience) on YouTube and other platforms, earning recognition from tech companies and scientific communities.
