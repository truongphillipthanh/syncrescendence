Unsolicited Advice is a popular YouTube channel and Substack blog created and run by Joe Folley, a philosopher from the United Kingdom. The channel is known for its "digestible philosophical videos" on a range of topics, including logic, nihilism, metaphysics, and ethics. The channel has grown to hundreds of thousands of subscribers and millions of total views. 

About Joe Folley
- Education: Folley earned both a bachelor's and a master's degree in philosophy from the University of Cambridge, where he specialized in logic.
- Career: After completing his education, he launched the Unsolicited Advice YouTube channel in April 2023. He also writes a related blog on the platform Substack.
- Online Presence: Folley brings his philosophical training to a public audience, producing content that is both educational and easy to understand. His work has been featured on other platforms, including in a discussion with fellow philosophy YouTuber Alex J. O'Connor.
- Views: Folley approaches complex philosophical subjects with nuance and humility. In a 2025 interview, he discussed his journey as an atheist philosopher, explaining why he is attracted to Christian faith but remains an atheist. 

Content and themes
- Core mission: Folley states that his aim is to "provide philosophy that people may find helpful". He encourages his audience to "take what works for you, and leave the rest".
- Topics covered:
    - Logic: He has a particular interest and specialization in the field of logic and has produced videos on topics like logical fallacies.
    - Metaphysics and consciousness: Folley explores the nature of reality and consciousness in videos with titles such as "What is Consciousness?".
    - Religion and ethics: He has discussed nihilism, the existence of God, and other questions concerning religion and the basis of morality.
- Audience interaction: Folley occasionally answers viewer questions, though in one video, he ironically noted that when he answers questions, the advice is technically "solicited".
