## PART II: SCOPE DOMAIN FRAMEWORK (Operational, Not Canonical)

**CRITICAL POSITIONING**: Scope Domain is operational guidance for time allocation, NOT a seventh canonical dimension. It belongs in Operations playbook (here) rather than cosmos-level taxonomy.

### Three Scope Domains

**Life Domain** — Personal Foundation
- Health maintenance (sleep, nutrition, exercise, medical care)
- Relationship cultivation (family, friends, intimate partnerships)
- Home environment management
- Financial baseline operations
- Personal development and interior work
- Rest, recovery, and restoration

**Development Domain** — Capability Building
- Chain-specific skill acquisition
- Framework study and internalization
- Practice protocol execution
- Experimentation and refinement
- AI-assisted learning acceleration
- Cross-domain synthesis development

**Demonstration Domain** — Value Delivery
- Public content production
- Teaching and transmission
- Consultation and professional services
- Community engagement and support
- Platform presence maintenance
- Economic value generation

---

### Time Allocation Across Domains

**Healthy Distribution** (Energy-Dependent):

**Peak Energy State** (70-100%):
- Life: 30-40% (foundation never neglected)
- Development: 30-40% (ambitious learning possible)
- Demonstration: 20-30% (creative output energizing)

**Baseline Energy State** (40-70%):
- Life: 40-50% (foundation prioritized)
- Development: 25-35% (moderate learning sustainable)
- Demonstration: 15-25% (routine production manageable)

**Depletion State** (20-40%):
- Life: 60-80% (recovery paramount)
- Development: 10-20% (maintenance only)
- Demonstration: 5-15% (minimal obligations only)

**Crisis State** (<20%):
- Life: 90-100% (survival mode)
- Development: 0% (paused completely)
- Demonstration: 0-10% (critical obligations only)

---

### Domain Pathologies (Warning Patterns)

**All Development, No Life** → Burnout
- Neglected health deteriorates
- Relationships atrophy
- Home environment chaos
- Foundation collapse inevitable
- **Recovery**: Minimum 3-6 months Life Domain prioritization

**All Demonstration, No Development** → Stagnation
- Content becomes repetitive
- Capability advancement stalls
- Framework ossifies rather than evolves
- Audience engagement declines
- **Recovery**: Reduce output 50%, redirect to Development

**All Life, No Development/Demonstration** → Isolation
- No value generation or contribution
- Economic sustainability impossible
- Community connection absent
- Meaning and purpose unclear
- **Recovery**: Start micro-commitments in Development, then gradually add Demonstration

**Balanced Integration** → Sustainable Excellence
- Life Domain provides stable foundation
- Development Domain generates increasing capability
- Demonstration Domain delivers value and economic sustainability
- Virtuous cycle: Better life enables more development, development improves demonstration quality, demonstration generates resources supporting life quality

---

### Phase-Specific Domain Emphasis

**Foundation Phase** (Months 1-24):
- Development: 40-50% (capability building primary)
- Life: 35-45% (foundation establishment)
- Demonstration: 10-20% (initial public presence)

**Validation Phase** (Months 25-48):
- Development: 35-40% (continued learning)
- Demonstration: 30-35% (scaling output)
- Life: 25-35% (maintaining foundation)

**Scaling Phase** (Months 49-72):
- Demonstration: 40-50% (professional output)
- Development: 25-30% (targeted advancement)
- Life: 20-30% (efficiency optimization)

**Institute Phase** (Months 73-96):
- Demonstration: 45-50% (teaching primary)
- Life: 25-30% (sustainable pace)
- Development: 20-25% (continuous refinement)

**Infrastructure Phase** (Months 97+):
- All three domains integrate (Field Node operations encompass life, development, and demonstration simultaneously)
- Traditional separation dissolves as physical infrastructure enables integration

## PART III: SEVEN PULSES PROTOCOL (Universal Foundation)

The Seven Pulses Dashboard serves all practitioners across all six dimensions. Daily health assessment operates regardless of Scale sophistication, Level advancement, Degree depth, Stage maturity, Phase timeline, or Modal focus.

### Morning Pulse Check (2 minutes)

**Pulse 1: Energy State** — Current capacity level?
- Physical vitality: Rested/Fatigued/Exhausted
- Cognitive clarity: Sharp/Functional/Foggy
- Emotional availability: Open/Neutral/Contracted
- Spiritual connection: Present/Distant/Absent
- Overall: Peak/Baseline/Depletion/Crisis

**Pulse 2: Foundation Alignment** — Grounded in values?
- Daily actions aligned with stated priorities?
- Living according to articulated principles?
- Integrity between belief and behavior?
- Green/Yellow/Red assessment

**Pulse 3: Direction Clarity** — Aimed true?
- Clear on immediate objectives?
- Trajectory toward meaningful goals?
- Purpose evident in daily choices?
- Green/Yellow/Red assessment

**Pulse 4: Rhythm Balance** — Life feels balanced?
- Work/rest cycles sustainable?
- Domain allocation appropriate (Life/Development/Demonstration)?
- No dimension collapsing under neglect?
- Green/Yellow/Red assessment

**Pulse 5: Practice Integrity** — Habits serving me?
- Daily practices supporting goals?
- Routines energizing rather than depleting?
- Systems functioning or requiring adjustment?
- Green/Yellow/Red assessment

**Pulse 6: Flow Presence** — Present in life?
- Engaged rather than distracted?
- Aware of moment-to-moment experience?
- Not lost in rumination or anxiety?
- Green/Yellow/Red assessment

**Pulse 7: Learning Trajectory** — Growing?
- Capability demonstrably advancing?
- New understanding emerging?
- Stagnation avoided through active development?
- Green/Yellow/Red assessment

---

### Midday Pulse Check (1 minute)

- Energy State re-assessment
- Alignment verification (actions matching morning intention?)
- Rhythm check (pace sustainable?)
- Course correction if needed

---

### Evening Pulse Check (1 minute)

- Energy State closing measurement
- Day quality holistic assessment
- Tomorrow planning based on energy projection
- Pattern recognition over time

---

### Weekly Synthesis (5 minutes)

**Sunday Evening Reflection**:
- Seven-day pattern review across all pulses
- Trending analysis (improving/stable/declining per pulse)
- Energy state patterns identification
- Scope Domain allocation assessment
- Operational adaptation planning
- Next week intentions setting

---

### Seven Pulses Scoring System

**Green = 3 points** (thriving, optimal, excellent)
**Yellow = 2 points** (adequate, functional, manageable)
**Red = 1 point** (struggling, challenged, requires attention)

**Daily Total**: 7-21 points possible
**Weekly Average**: Track trends over time
**Monthly Patterns**: Identify systemic issues vs. temporary fluctuations

**Baseline Establishment** (Week 1):
- Calculate average daily score
- This becomes personal baseline for comparison
- Trending above baseline = improvement
- Trending below baseline = attention required

---

### Energy State to Practice Matching

**Peak State** (70-100%):
- Seven Pulses + ambitious chain development (30-60 min)
- Complex problem-solving energizing
- Creative projects accessible
- Teaching/transmission possible
- Demonstration Domain work thriving

**Baseline State** (40-70%):
- Seven Pulses + moderate development (15-30 min)
- Routine maintenance sustainable
- Standard production manageable
- Balanced domain allocation possible

**Depletion State** (20-40%):
- Seven Pulses only (2 min)
- Optional micro-commitment (single small action)
- Life Domain prioritization
- Zero Development or Demonstration pressure
- Maximum self-compassion required

**Crisis State** (<20%):
- Practice PAUSED (zero guilt)
- Rest prioritized over all tracking
- Professional support if persists beyond 3-5 days
- Framework suspended until >30% energy returns
- Life Domain exclusively (survival mode)

**CRITICAL**: Practicing through depletion or crisis creates burnout and framework resentment. Honor energy state always. The framework serves the practitioner, not vice versa.

## PART VI: ENERGY-AWARE OPERATIONAL PROTOCOLS

### Universal Neurodivergent-Informed Practices

### Burnout Prevention (Constitutional Requirement)

**Early Warning Signs**:
- Seven Pulses declining trend across multiple dimensions
- Energy State chronically in Depletion (30-40%) for 2+ weeks
- Resentment toward practices previously enjoyable
- Procrastination increasing systematically
- Sleep disruption or quality degradation
- Social withdrawal and isolation
- Anhedonia (loss of joy in activities)
- Increased irritability and emotional reactivity

**Immediate Interventions**:
1. **Pause Development and Demonstration** (Life Domain exclusively)
2. **Reduce commitments by 50-75%** (renegotiate deadlines, cancel optional engagements)
3. **Sleep optimization priority** (8-9 hours minimum, quality focus)
4. **Physical movement daily** (gentle, not intense—walks, yoga, swimming)
5. **Social connection with safe people** (vulnerability possible, no performance required)
6. **Professional support if persists** (therapist, coach, physician)

**Recovery Timeline**: 
- Minimum 3-6 weeks Life Domain prioritization
- Gradual reintroduction of Development Domain (micro-commitments only)
- Demonstration Domain last to resume (after sustainable Development practice reestablished)

**System Redesign Required**:
- Burnout indicates system miscalibration, not personal failure
- Something must change: Reduce total volume, adjust domain allocation, eliminate energy-draining commitments
- Cannot return to exact previous system—it produced burnout

---

### Capacity Oscillation Management

**Recognition**: Capacity isn't constant. Weekly, monthly, and seasonal fluctuations are normal and expected.

**Weekly Patterns**:
- Many practitioners have high-capacity days (Tuesday-Thursday) and low-capacity days (Monday, Friday, weekends)
- Schedule accordingly: Ambitious development on high-capacity days, maintenance on low-capacity days

**Monthly Patterns**:
- Hormonal cycles affect capacity significantly (for practitioners with menstrual cycles)
- Energy often higher in follicular phase, lower in luteal/menstrual phases
- Adjust expectations and commitments according to predictable patterns

**Seasonal Patterns**:
- Winter often brings lower capacity (especially in northern latitudes—light exposure matters)
- Summer frequently enables higher capacity
- Fall/Spring transitions can disrupt patterns temporarily

**Protocol**: Track patterns over 3-6 months, identify personal rhythms, schedule major projects during predictable high-capacity periods, reduce commitments during predictable low-capacity periods.

### Executive Function Demand Tracking

**Cognitive Load Categories**:

**Low Executive Function Demand**:
- Routine maintenance tasks
- Previously mastered procedures
- Consumption and learning (passive intake)
- Physical movement and exercise

**Moderate Executive Function Demand**:
- Novel synthesis and content creation
- IIC reconnaissance and curation
- Strategic planning and decision-making
- Teaching and transmission (once mastered)

**High Executive Function Demand**:
- Framework development and conceptual architecture

**Energy State Matching**:
- Peak State: High executive function tasks accessible
- Baseline State: Moderate executive function tasks manageable
- Depletion State: Low executive function tasks only
- Crisis State: Zero executive function tasks (rest exclusively)

**Protocol**: Assess task executive function demand before committing. Decline high-demand tasks during Baseline or Depletion states rather than accepting and failing. This prevents commitment debt accumulation.

- Abandon framework: Not resonating after genuine sustained effort—okay to pivot to different work
