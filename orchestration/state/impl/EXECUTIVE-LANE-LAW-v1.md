# Executive Lane Law v1

**Date**: 2026-03-02  
**Status**: constitutional redesign package  
**Purpose**: define the lawful functions of executive sublanes inside the successor shell

## 0. Why This Exists

The old `-SOVEREIGN` lane carried real executive value:

- briefings
- state-of-the-union syntheses
- summit reviews
- escalations

What failed was not the executive function.
What failed was mixing those functions into one shell-hostile dumping lane.

## 1. Executive Sublanes

The successor shell therefore distinguishes:

- [executive/briefings](/Users/system/syncrescendence/executive/briefings)
- [executive/escalations](/Users/system/syncrescendence/executive/escalations)
- [executive/summits](/Users/system/syncrescendence/executive/summits)

## 2. Briefings

Briefings are for:

- state-of-the-union style synthesis
- executive snapshots
- sovereign-facing cross-lane summaries

Briefings are not:

- backlog items
- raw prompts/responses
- handoffs

## 3. Escalations

Escalations are for:

- unresolved executive-level decisions
- conflicts between lanes
- blocked conditions that local offices cannot lawfully resolve

Escalations are not:

- office-local uncertainty
- private scratch work
- generic complaints

## 4. Summits

Summits are for:

- cabinet-style subsystem review
- comparative assessment across ministries or lanes
- synchronized executive checkpoints

Summits are not:

- ordinary standups
- raw implementation reports

## 5. Promotion Rules

Executive artifacts should be promoted when:

1. local office work crosses into sovereign steering
2. multiple lanes need joint review
3. a briefing, escalation, or summit result changes intent or program truth

## 6. Anti-Patterns

Forbidden:

1. reviving `-SOVEREIGN`-style mixed dumping
2. storing prompts or responses directly as executive artifacts without classification
3. using executive lanes as archive bins
4. using executive lanes to bypass communications or program law
