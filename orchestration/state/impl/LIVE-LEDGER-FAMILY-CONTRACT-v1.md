# Live Ledger Family Contract v1

**Status**: live-v1
**Class**: implementation law
**Purpose**: define the shell's generic live-ledger family so mutable intelligence stays lawful across sovereignty, substrate, synthesis, and dashboard or projection layers

## Compact Contract

- repo ratifies meaning, schema law, and allowed reliance
- substrate records append-only events, receipts, and observations
- synthesis derives reusable snapshots from substrate under ratified rules
- dashboard and projection surfaces expose current state without becoming sovereign

Nothing direction-bearing becomes shell truth unless repo ratification authorizes the field family, derivation rule, and reliance class.

## Layering Law

| Layer | Required burden | Primary residence | Forbidden drift |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sovereignty | family law, domain contract, schema law, materialization law, ratification pointers, and allowed reliance classes | repo | dashboards, registries, or graph shape becoming hidden authority |
| Substrate | append-only events, receipts, provenance, contradictions, and live mutations | exocortex by default | current-state-only mutation, silent rewrite, or untraceable compaction |
| Synthesis | deterministic reconciliation, compaction, and snapshot candidates with explicit lineage back to substrate | exocortex first, repo on ratification | analysis that erases provenance or silently invents new law |
| Dashboard / Projection | human-resolution views, typed projections, and visible status surfaces over lawful upstream state | exocortex and ontology | becoming the source of truth or the only home of meaning |

Dashboard and projection surfaces are optional consumers of the family.
They may repeat and organize live-ledger state, but they may not replace the sovereign or substrate layers.

## State Surface Law

Every mutable ledger family must define two linked surfaces:

1. an append-only substrate
2. a current-state surface

Rules:

1. The append-only substrate is the historical witness for meaningful observations and mutations.
2. The current-state surface is an operational convenience layer for the latest lawful state.
3. The current-state surface may be rebuilt, replaced, or rematerialized, but only from append-only substrate plus repo-ratified derivation rules.
4. No operationally material fact may exist only in current state.
5. No append-only event may be silently rewritten after reliance has attached to it; corrections must arrive as new events or explicit supersession records.
6. If current state disagrees with substrate or cannot be deterministically rebuilt, current state becomes informative only until repaired.
7. Repo-ratified snapshots may compact conclusions from live state, but they do not erase substrate history.

Append-only describes the ledger law, not a storage vendor.
The requirement is traceable mutation history, not any one implementation technology.

## Repo Versus Exocortex Burden Split

Repo must hold:

- family law
- domain-specific contract law
- schema and field-family law
- materialization and ratification rules
- approved synthesis snapshots worth future session loading

Exocortex should hold:

- high-churn append-only substrate events
- live current-state materializations
- transient reconciliation and analysis entries before ratification
- dashboard views and operational projections

Ontology may hold:

- typed projections over repo and exocortex state
- lineage, relation, and contradiction visibility

Ontology may not hold:

- the only copy of authority-bearing meaning
- hidden field-family invention
- unratified semantic upgrades

## Default And Exception

The widening default is:

`repo-ratified law -> exocortex append-only substrate -> derived current-state surface -> repo-ratified synthesis snapshot -> optional dashboard or projection surface`

A ledger family may remain repo-native only when its live burden is low-churn, authority-bearing, and still tractable without creating hidden operational state outside the repo.
That is an exception path, not the family default.

## Out Of Scope For v1

This artifact does not define:

- widening gates
- freshness or decay adjunct contracts
- standalone validator specifications
- per-domain manifests or rollout order

Those may be ratified later as separate contracts if the family proves stable.
