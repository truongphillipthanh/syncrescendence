# Don't Die: Bryan Johnson on Living Forever, AI, and Blueprint's $60M Round

## Executive Summary

Bryan Johnson—founder of Braintree/Venmo, now Blueprint—presents the "Don't Die" philosophy as both civilizational positioning and commercial strategy. The $60M raise marks mainstream acceptance of longevity as a category. The deeper thesis: Blueprint is "entirely about AI alignment"—caring for your body is caring for intelligence, and the coming autonomous health systems will run on your behalf as AI matures. A Western thought macro-cycle is ending; we're overdue for a paradigm shift from capitalism-as-scarcity to purpose-as-abundance.

## Key Insights

### "Don't Die" as Philosophy, Not Slogan
Saying yes to "don't die" means: "I value existence, I respect existence." The endeavor is "entirely about AI alignment"—when you care for yourself, you care for your body, when you care for your body, you care for intelligence. It's a holistic acknowledgment of existence at this pivotal moment.

### Autonomous Health Systems
Karpathy's autonomous systems framing applied to health: currently we chase down food, protocols, evidence ourselves. In the future, "it will just be run for us." Blueprint is building autonomous health infrastructure—digital, food, prescriptions, testing—that rides scientific breakthroughs as they emerge. "We are going to build your autonomous self."

### Macro Frame: End of Western Thought Era
Aristotle → Christianity → Medieval → Renaissance → Enlightenment → Modern Science → [?]. We're at the tail end, overdue for a major shift. Capitalism was for solving scarcity; we're now in compulsive consumption. Democracy was freedom of choice; we're now addicted and choiceless. Sleep and exercise matter, but the larger frame is "what it means to be intelligent, make decisions. You can't when you're addicted to everything."

### Business Model: Collapse the Category
Blueprint = "home base for longevity, win the early innings, then ride the multi-trillion dollar market." Like Braintree/Stripe collapsing payments, Blueprint aims to collapse the fragmented health/longevity space into a unified, evidence-based, biomarker-led experience. "Once you collapse the entire space, it's very hard to get in."

### AGI Definition via Psychology
When will AGI arrive? Johnson's framing: "When will humans feel useless?" The psychological transition point where AI does everything so well that humans face existential purposelessness. Karpathy's vision (gym + learning) may persist, but without "the tension of stress and real life," leisure becomes uncomfortable. "Competing on biomarkers" may become the final status game.
