Curt Jaimungal is a Canadian podcaster, filmmaker, and presenter known for hosting the popular YouTube podcast Theories of Everything. A mathematical physicist by training, he explores complex subjects at the intersection of science, philosophy, and consciousness with leading experts. 

Education and early career
- Jaimungal is a Torontonian who graduated from the University of Toronto with a degree in Mathematical Physics.
- His early professional background includes work in filmmaking and stand-up comedy, skills he later incorporates into his podcast style.
- In 2021, Jaimungal co-directed the documentary film Better Left Unsaid, which analyzes political extremism, focusing heavily on the extreme left. 

The Theories of Everythingpodcast
- Launched in 2020, Jaimungal's podcast, Theories of Everything (TOE), gained a large following for its in-depth, rigorous, and open-minded discussions.
- Focus areas: The podcast delves into theoretical physics, consciousness, artificial intelligence, cosmology, free will, and the nature of reality.
- Interview style: Jaimungal is known for his contemplative and detailed interviews. He encourages guests to deeply elaborate on their viewpoints without "dumbing down" the concepts for the audience, often paralleling the intensity of academic discourse.
- Notable guests: He has interviewed a wide range of distinguished figures, including Sean Carroll, Noam Chomsky, Eric Weinstein, Edward Frenkel, and Bryan Johnson. 

Later work and recognition
- In 2024, Jaimungal was named a Fellow of O'Shaughnessy Ventures for his work on the podcast. The organization's goal is to help him expand the TOE platform into an interdisciplinary research hub.
- He has expanded his work to include personal writings and early access content via a Substack.
- He also continues to publish content on his YouTube channel, which features his podcast episodes and other videos exploring complex topics.
