The a16z Podcast is the official podcast of Andreessen Horowitz, a prominent Silicon Valley-based venture capital firm. The network of shows discusses technology, innovation, and culture with the "builders" behind the most important ideas in tech. The podcast aims to help listeners stay ahead of the curve, covering a wide range of topics from AI, crypto, and fintech to consumer tech, healthcare, and infrastructure.
Episodes feature a mix of venture capital partners, founders, industry experts, researchers, and other notable thinkers, providing insights into the future of technology and how it is changing all aspects of life.
Hosts of the a16z Podcast
As a network of shows, the a16z Podcast does not have a single host but rather features a rotating cast of Andreessen Horowitz partners, who often host episodes related to their specific areas of expertise. Notable hosts include:
- Marc Andreessen: Co-founder of Andreessen Horowitz, an internet pioneer, and a frequent host on "The Ben and Marc Show" and other special segments.
- Ben Horowitz: Co-founder of Andreessen Horowitz and a regular presence on the podcast, discussing business strategy, leadership, and startup culture.
- Martin Casado: A General Partner focusing on AI and enterprise software, known for his deep dives into AI and infrastructure.
- Anish Acharya: A General Partner specializing in consumer tech, who regularly explores new consumer trends and technologies.
- Katherine Boyle: A General Partner who focuses on the "American Dynamism" thesis, investing in companies that support the national interest across sectors like defense and energy.
- Angela Strange: A General Partner covering fintech, who frequently hosts discussions on financial services and the future of banking.
- Connie Chan: A General Partner who specializes in consumer technology, particularly on emerging behaviors and models.
- Sonal Chokshi: Formerly the editor-in-chief, she was a longtime host and voice on the podcast network.
- Steph Smith: A frequent host who has covered a wide variety of "Big Ideas" in tech and hosts conversations with developers and founders.
- Erik Torenberg: A General Partner who frequently hosts segments exploring broad technology trends with Marc Andreessen and other prominent tech figures.
