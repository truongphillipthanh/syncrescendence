Quanta Magazine is an editorially independent, online publication dedicated to covering developments in basic research in mathematics, physics, biology, and computer science. Known for its in-depth, high-quality journalism, the magazine aims to enhance public understanding of complex scientific and mathematical concepts through clear storytelling. 

Background
- Founder: Thomas Lin, a former journalist for The New York Times, founded Quanta Magazine in 2012. The publication was initially called Simons Science News and was renamed in 2013.
- Parent Organization: Quanta Magazine was launched and is funded by the Simons Foundation, a non-profit organization established by Jim and Marilyn Simons to advance research in mathematics and the basic sciences.
- Editorial Independence: Despite its funding, Quanta maintains complete editorial independence. Its content is not influenced by the foundation's views or grant-giving activities. 

Mission and journalistic approach
- Focus on basic science: The magazine specializes in reporting on fundamental research at the frontiers of knowledge, steering away from "quick, snackable content" and sensationalism.
- Storytelling: Articles are crafted to make challenging subjects accessible to a broad audience without oversimplification, weaving together complex science with the art of storytelling.
- Rigorous journalism: The publication emphasizes meticulous reporting, editing, and fact-checking to ensure accuracy. 

Features and content
- Wide coverage: Quanta publishes news articles, features, interviews, and columns on a range of topics, including cosmology, particle physics, evolutionary biology, and theoretical computer science.
- Multimedia: In addition to written content, Quanta produces multimedia features, such as videos, podcasts, and interactive graphics, to explain complex topics. The popular podcast series "The Joy of x" features conversations with brilliant scientific and mathematical minds.
- Syndication: To reach a wider audience, Quanta has content-sharing agreements with other prominent publications like Scientific American, Wired, and The Atlantic.
- Books: The magazine also publishes book collections of its articles through partnerships with publishers like The MIT Press. 

Notable accolades
- Pulitzer Prize: The staff of Quanta, notably writer Natalie Wolchover, was awarded the 2022 Pulitzer Prize for Explanatory Reporting.
- National Magazine Awards: The publication has received numerous awards, including National Magazine Awards for General Excellence and Best Single-Topic Issue.
- Webby Awards: Quanta Magazine has won several Webby Awards for its website and writing.
