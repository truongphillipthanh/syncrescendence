The All-In Podcast features "besties" and venture capitalists Chamath Palihapitiya, Jason Calacanis, David Sacks, and David Friedberg discussing the latest news and trends across technology, business, politics, and society. What began as a remote, weekly poker game during the pandemic has become one of the most-listened-to business podcasts in the world.
The hosts are known for their candid and often provocative discussions, offering insider perspectives shaped by their decades of experience in Silicon Valley.
Host Bios
Chamath Palihapitiya
- The Dictator: Founder and CEO of Social Capital, a technology holding company that has incubated and invested in firms like Yammer, Slack, and Groq.
- Background: A former senior executive at Facebook who led its Growth, Platform, and Mobile teams.
- Investing philosophy: Focuses on backing innovative companies in sectors often overlooked by traditional venture capital, including healthcare, climate change, and artificial intelligence.
Jason Calacanis
- The Moderator: A serial internet entrepreneur, author, and prominent angel investor.
- Portfolio: Has invested in hundreds of early-stage startups, including four that have achieved "unicorn" status.
- Media Ventures: Also hosts the podcast This Week in Startups and founded LAUNCH Media, which organizes the LAUNCH Festival, one of the world's largest startup conferences.
David Sacks
- The Rainman: Entrepreneur, investor, and General Partner at Craft Ventures.
- PayPal Mafia Member: Served as the founding COO of PayPal and was a co-founder and CEO of Yammer, which was acquired by Microsoft for $1.2 billion.
- Product Expertise: Known for his product acumen and pioneering "Bottom-Up SaaS" strategy.
David Friedberg
- The Sultan: Founder and CEO of The Production Board (TPB), a venture foundry that builds and invests in businesses at the intersection of technology and life sciences.
- Exits: Founded The Climate Corporation, which was acquired by Monsanto for over $1 billion, and was an early executive at Google.
- Astrophysics Background: His deep scientific knowledge allows him to take a long-term, optimistic view on solving some of humanity's biggest problems, from agriculture to food production.
