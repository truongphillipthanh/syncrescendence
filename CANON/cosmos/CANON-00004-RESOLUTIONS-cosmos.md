### NEW IRRESOLUTION 2: Developmental Level Reification

**STATUS: PRODUCTIVELY HELD + EMPIRICAL TESTING NEEDED**

**The Tension**: Four-Level architecture (Initial → Intermediate → Advanced → Mastery) per chain may reify continuous development into discrete categories. Levels may be designer convenience rather than natural kinds.

**Honest Assessment**:  
Developmental Levels likely represent **useful approximation** rather than discovered natural kinds. Evidence for discrete stages is **mixed** in developmental psychology. Framework may be **imposing** levels rather than **discovering** them.

Real human development might be:
- Continuous rather than discrete (smooth curves, not stages)
- Domain-specific without general progression (expert in one micro-skill, novice in adjacent)
- Context-dependent (competent in supportive environments, collapsing under stress)
- Non-linear (regression common, spiraling rather than climbing)
- Culturally constructed (stages reflecting framework expectations, not reality)

**Evidence For Level-Like Progression**:
- Practitioners report qualitative shifts (not just quantitative improvement)
- Certain capabilities cluster together (bundled competencies)
- Consolidation periods observable (plateaus before breakthroughs)
- Teaching capacity emerges at specific thresholds

**Evidence Against Level-Like Progression**:
- Individual variation enormous (some skip "stages," others combine)
- Regression frequent (practitioners move backward under stress)
- Micro-skills develop independently (fine-grained, not coarse levels)
- Cross-cultural evidence mixed (stage models may be WEIRD bias)

**Modal Context Integration**:
- **Modal 1 (Abstraction)**: Text-based learning may show level-like progression
- **Modal 2 (Simulation)**: Visual practice patterns may differ
- **Modal 3 (Embodiment)**: Physical skill acquisition has established stage research
- **Modal 4 (Transcendence)**: Network coordination may reveal different developmental patterns

Levels may be more "real" in some modals than others. Physical skill stages (Embodiment) have more empirical support than abstract reasoning stages (Abstraction).

**Productive Tension Navigation**:  
Hold Levels **lightly as heuristic** rather than **rigidly as ontology**:
- Levels = navigational aids for practitioners
- Not claims about developmental reality
- Useful for communication and assessment
- Open to revision based on evidence
- Accept plurality of developmental pathways

**Resolution Pathway**:
1. Collect longitudinal developmental data across practitioners
2. Test whether level models fit data better than continuous models
3. Allow for individual variability (some follow levels, others don't)
4. Document level-transitions qualitatively (what actually changes?)
5. Revise level architecture if evidence demands
6. Accept if levels remain approximate rather than precise

**Falsification Criteria**:
- If developmental trajectories show no clustering around proposed Levels → level model invalid
- If practitioners transition smoothly without consolidation periods → development continuous, not level-like
- If regression is norm rather than exception → linear level model inappropriate
- If Level assignments show no predictive validity → levels arbitrary distinctions
- If Modal 2+ data contradicts Modal 1 patterns → modal-specific developmental structures

**Status**: PRODUCTIVELY HELD. Framework uses levels pragmatically while remaining epistemically humble about their ontological status. Empirical testing across modals essential.

**Cross-Reference**: See individual chain documents (CANON-30000 through CANON-35000) for Level specifications per chain
